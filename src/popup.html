Features and Tricks

    ★ HOVER on a word  to reveal its original English word.

    ★ CLICK on a word  to rotate through matching translated words. There may be false translations, so click through to find a translation you like.

    ★ CLICK+HOLD on a word  to access its detailed information page through Wiktionary.



    1st slider ("Use up to 6% of dictionary"):
    Changes the range of vocabulary you want to translate.
    100% means to use all 4,000 words in the vocab list.
    I recommend starting low and working up.

2nd slider ("Ignore the first 2% of dictionary"):
    Allows you to ignore earlier words that you're comfortable with already.
    100% means ignore every vocabulary word, 25% means ignoring the first quarter of words, and 0% means to not ignore any.

3rd slider ("See fewer/more words on page"):
    Affects how often you see translated words without altering the set of words you want translated.
    0% means no word on the site will be translated. 100% means every vocabulary word found on the site will be translated.
    I recommend setting it so that only a few words per paragraph are changed.

    Instead of showing translated words, game mode has you type in a correct translation instead.

    The blurred English word will follow the word input box if you need a hint.

    A word matching any translation will be labelled as correct.

    You may choose to require exact accents and umlauts etc., or you can go by a rough englishization if you'd like.

    For example, if enabled, 'ä' must match 'ä'. If disabled, 'ä' may also match 'a'.

About

    Thank you for downloading! While you browse as normal, random words will be automatically translated into your target language. To begin, all you need to do is select a language. I recommend pinning the extension's icon to your browser.

    Use this app as an accompaniment to other language lessons. It is nothing more than a flashcard app integrated into your web browser. You will be shown foreign words in the context of an English sentence, enabling you to use the sentence's meaning to familiarize yourself with the meaning of the foreign word.

    Some languages are better supported than others, based on the community on wiktionary.org. The most popular a language, the more vocabulary words are available. Most languages on here do not actually have 4,300 vocab words. Old English, for example, has only 93 (but I still provide it because it's silly).

    While I've tried my best, there will still be many words translated incorrectly given the sentence. It is up to you to differentiate good from poor translations. The quality of this app is not gauranteed, as the translations for the dozen+ languages were algorithmically prepared, and not necessarily checked by hand. Do not attempt to use this app to learn grammar.

    The translated words come from a dictionary which uses the first 4000 most common words ordered by frequency. As you progress through the dictionary, rarer words are encountered.

    The slider bars referring to "percent of dictionary" are warped, they don't refer to real percentages. Because earlier words are more common, 1% on the slider early on will activate maybe a dozen words, and will be seen on almost every page you visit. Likewise, less common words will rarely be seen. Therefore, 1% on the slider later on activates hundreds of words. This is so that your webpages will at least present some words for you to work on.

    This version uses a unigram language model for greedy translations. Future versions could include n-gram models or in browser neural models if the performance is good enough.
