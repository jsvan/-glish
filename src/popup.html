-glish








    There is a 5% chance to translate words from a vocabulary list with 0 unique words in it.

Features and Tricks

    ★ HOVER on a word  to reveal its original English word.

    ★ CLICK on a word  to rotate through matching translated words. There may be false translations, so click through to find a translation you like.

    ★ CLICK+HOLD on a word  to access its detailed information page through Wiktionary.


    The 1st slider ("Use up to the first 6% of dictionary"), changes the range of vocabulary you want to translate. 100% means to use all 4,000 words in the vocab list. I recommend starting low and working your way up.

    The 2nd slider ("Ignore the first 2% of dictionary"), allows you to ignore earlier words that you're comfortable with already. 0% means use entire dictionary, 25% means ignoring the first quarter of the vocab words, and 100% means ignore everything.

    The 3rd slider ("Convert words with a 20% chance"), affects how often you see translated words without altering the set of words you want translated. 0% means no words will be converted. 100% means every found vocabulary word will be converted. I recommend setting it so that only a few words per paragraph are changed.

About

    Thank you for downloading! While you browse as normal, random words will be automatically translated into your target language. To begin, all you need to do is select a language. I recommend pinning the extension's icon to your browser.

    Use this app as an accompaniment to other language lessons. It is nothing more than a flashcard app integrated into your web browser. You will be shown foreign words in the context of an English sentence, enabling you to use the sentence's meaning to familiarize yourself with the meaning of the foreign word.
    While I've tried my best, there will still be many words translated incorrectly given the sentence. It is up to you to differentiate good from poor translations. The quality of this app is not gauranteed, as the translations for the dozen+ languages were algorithmically prepared, and not necessarily checked by hand. Do not attempt to use this app to learn grammar.

    The translated words come from a dictionary which uses the first 4000 most common words ordered by frequency. As you progress through the dictionary, rarer words are encountered.

    The slider bars refering to "percent of dictionary" are warped, and don't refer to real percentages. Because earlier words are more common, 1% on the slider early on will activate maybe a dozen words, and will be seen on almost every page you visit.
    Likewise, less common words will rarely be seen. Therefore, 1% on the slider later on activates hundreds of words. This is so that your webpages will at least present some words for you to work on.

    This version uses a unigram language model for greedy translations. Future versions could include n-gram models or in browser neural models if the performance is good enough.
